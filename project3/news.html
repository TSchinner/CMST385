The unofficial break-up of the Beatles happened in 1970 when the debut album of Paul's new band was released, Wings, WildLife. But it took until December 1974 for John Lennon to sign the court documents that officially disbanded the Beatles. All four group members went on to successful solo careers.

In December 1980, John Lennon was gunned down by a disturbed fan in front of his apartment building in New York City. This event, supposedly, ended all hope of the original four Beatles getting back together to make more music. But in 1995 the three remaining members of the band got together with 3 demo tapes John recorded before his death and created 2 new songs. "Free as a Bird" and "Real Love" were released as part of a three, double album, anthology, featuring the music of recording sessions throughout the member's time as Beatles in the '60s

In November 2001, George Harrison lost his battle with lung cancer and passed away at the age of 58. This left Paul and Ringo as the last two surviving Beatles. In, 2002, they both reentered the studio armed with the last demo of a song John recorded before he died, recordings of the work George did on John's demo song in 1995, during their Anthology sessions, and the hope that artificial intelligence could isolate John's voice on his demo song so it could be used to recreate one last Beatles song.
